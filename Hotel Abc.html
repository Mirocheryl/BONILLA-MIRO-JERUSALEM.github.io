Our Restaurant

Welcome to Casa Del Sol Restaurant, where culinary excellence meets warm hospitality. Nestled in the heart of Oslob, our restaurant is a place where flavors, aromas, and memories come together to create a truly unforgettable dining experience. Whether you're seeking a romantic dinner for two, a celebratory meal with friends and family, or a business lunch with colleagues, we are here to serve you.

Italian Kitchen

Lasagna: Layers of pasta, ricotta cheese, marinara sauce, ground meat (usually beef or pork), and melted mozzarella cheese, baked to perfection.
Margherita Pizza: A simple pizza topped with tomato sauce, fresh mozzarella cheese, basil leaves, and a drizzle of olive oil.
Risotto: Creamy Italian rice dish cooked with broth and flavored with ingredients such as mushrooms, seafood, or saffron.
Osso Buco: A Milanese specialty featuring braised veal shanks cooked with white wine, broth, and gremolata (a mixture of lemon zest, garlic, and parsley).
Tiramisu: A classic Italian dessert made with layers of coffee-soaked ladyfingers and mascarpone cheese, dusted with cocoa powder.

Mexican Kitchen

Tacos:Soft or crispy tortillas filled with a variety of ingredients, including seasoned meats, salsa, guacamole, and sour cream.
Enchiladas: Tortillas rolled around a filling (often shredded chicken or beef), topped with chili sauce and cheese, then baked until bubbly.
Guacamole: A dip made from mashed avocados, often mixed with lime juice, onions, tomatoes, and spices.
Chiles Rellenos: Poblano peppers stuffed with cheese, meat, or beans, dipped in egg batter, and fried.
Churros: Fried dough pastry sticks, often dusted with cinnamon sugar and served with chocolate dipping sauce.

Philippine Kitchen

Adobo: A popular dish featuring meat (chicken, pork, or seafood) marinated and simmered in a mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and spices.
Sinigang: A sour tamarind-based soup with a variety of vegetables and protein like shrimp or pork.
Pancit Canton: A stir-fried noodle dish with vegetables, shrimp, and sliced meat.
Lechon: A whole roasted pig, often prepared for special occasions and celebrations.
Halo-Halo: A Filipino dessert made with crushed ice, sweetened fruits, jellies, and beans, topped with leche flan, purple yam (ube), and more.

Chinese Kitchen

Kung Pao Chicken: A stir-fried dish with diced chicken, peanuts, and vegetables in a spicy, savory sauce.
Dim Sum: A variety of small, flavorful dishes often served in bamboo steamer baskets, including dumplings, buns, and rolls.
Peking Duck: A famous dish featuring roasted duck with crispy skin, served with thin pancakes, hoisin sauce, and thinly sliced scallions.
Mongolian Beef: Sliced beef stir-fried with scallions and a savory brown sauce.
Hot and Sour Soup: A spicy and tangy soup with ingredients like tofu, mushrooms, and bamboo shoots.
